# Code of Conduct

Vigla uses a simple contribution standard: be direct, respectful, and focused on the work.

## Expected Behavior

- Treat contributors with respect regardless of experience level, background, employer, language, or tool preference.
- Critique code, designs, and tradeoffs rather than people.
- Assume good intent, but accept correction when impact and intent differ.
- Keep discussions relevant to the issue or pull request.
- Make space for newcomers by answering basic project questions without gatekeeping.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, slurs, or personal attacks
- Sexualized language or unwelcome attention
- Doxing or publishing private information
- Repeated derailing of technical discussions
- Bad-faith participation, spam, or intentionally disruptive behavior

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments; close issues; reject contributions; or temporarily/permanently restrict participation when behavior harms the project.

Enforcement should be proportional and transparent where possible. For minor issues, maintainers should prefer a clear correction. For severe or repeated issues, maintainers may act immediately.

## Reporting

If you see behavior that violates this code, open a confidential report through
this repository's **Security → Report a vulnerability** tab and begin the title
with `Conduct report:`. It reaches only the maintainers and is the project's
accepted private channel for conduct reports as well as vulnerabilities.
Include links, screenshots, dates, and a short description of what happened.
Do not file the report as a public issue. Maintainers should avoid public
back-and-forth unless the reporter asks for it or public action is necessary.

If a report concerns the sole maintainer, or you need an independent appeal
about conduct hosted on GitHub, use [GitHub Support's abuse-reporting
channel](https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse). Vigla cannot promise
independent internal adjudication while it has one maintainer; that limitation
and the path for adding maintainers are documented in
[GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md). Do not send vulnerability details through a
conduct report.
